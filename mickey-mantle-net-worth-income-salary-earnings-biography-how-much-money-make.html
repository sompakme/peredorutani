Mickey Charles Mantle (October 20, 1931 – August 13, 1995), sometimes known as “The Commerce Comet” or “The Mick,” was an American professional baseball player who played with the New York Yankees from 1931 to 199Over the course of 18 seasons with the New York Yankees in Major League Baseball (MLB), he played centerfield and first base for the team. Many consider Mantle to be the greatest switch hitter in baseball history, as well as one of the best players in the sport’s history overall. Among his many accomplishments, Mantle was inducted into the National Baseball Hall of Fame in 1974 and named to the Major League Baseball All-Century Team in 199Mantle was renowned for his ability to hit for both average and power, particularly with tape-measure home runs, and for his ability to bat for both average and power. In 1956, he won the Triple Crown by leading the Major League Baseball in batting average, home runs, and runs batted in (RBI). He was awarded American League (AL) Most Valuable Player (MVP) three times and was selected to the All-Star team sixteen times, appearing in 19 of the 20 All-Star games to which he was selected. Mantle participated in 12 World Series games, with his club winning seven of them under his leadership. He owns the World Series records for the most home runs (18), RBIs (40), runs (42), walks (43), extra-base hits (26), and total bases (101), among other statistics (123). Additionally, he is the all-time leader (tied with Jim Thome) in walk-off home runs, having hit a total of thirteen in his career, twelve during the regular season and one during the playoffs.
