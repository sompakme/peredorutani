WIC Program Benefits: How many stores accept WIC card?

Low-income pregnant, breastfeeding, and non-breastfeeding postpartum women as well as infants and children up to age five who are found to be at nutritional risk could benefit from the Special Supplemental Nutrition Program for Women, Infants, and Children (WIC).

This scheme provides federal grants to states for supplemental foods, health care referrals, and nutrition education. State agencies are responsible for authorizing vendors, determining eligibility and providing benefits and services across the United States.

The Food and Nutrition Service of the U.S. Department of Agriculture oversees WIC on a federal level. It was first implemented as a pilot program in 1972 and was declared permanent in 1974. The Healthy Meals for Healthy Americans Act of 1994 changed its name to emphasize its function as a nutrition program.

Can you use WIC at Walgreens

Walgreens has stores across the country, which means that it depends on whether your state has approved the local Walgreens as a WIC retailer. To check if you can USE WIC at your Walgreens, you can contact the store and kindly ask for more information.

Does Amazon accept WIC

According to the US Department of Agriculture, WIC recipients are not allowed to use their benefits online. However, there are some states which are currently trying to move in that direction.

As for Amazon, they offer WIC recipients a discount for Amazon Prime, but they can't use the benefits as an online payment through their website.

Can you use WIC online at Walmart

Unfortunately, although Walmart accepts WIC payments at all their stores across the United States, there is no online shopping option for WIC recipients.

Who is eligible for the program?

To be eligible for WIC, applicants must live in the state in which they apply and be either pregnant, or breastfeeding, or postpartum, or a child five years old or younger.

A person automatically qualifies for benefits if they take part in or have family members who take part in certain other benefit programs, such as SNAP, Medicaid, or TANF.
